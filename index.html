Bob Paisley

The man who conquered all of Europe with Liverpool FC

Bob Paisley's personal life timeline:

  • 23.01.1919 - Robert(Bob) Paisley is born in Hetton-le-Hole, England.
  • 1939 - Paisley wins his first trophy
  • 08.05.1939 - Signs his professional form with Liverpool FC
  • 16.03.1940 - Wartime debut versus Manchester United FC
  • World War II - serves as an anti tank gunner with Montgomery's Eighth Army
  • 05.01.1946 - first official appearance for Liverpool FC.
  • 17.07.1946 - Marries his wife Jessie.
  • 13.03.1954 - Plays his last game for Liverpool in a 3-1 loss to Sheffield United.

Bob Paisley's manager career timeline:

  • 1954 - Paisley becomes the trainer of Liverpool's Central League side and physio.
  • 01.05.1957 - Paisley takes over first team coache duties as Phil Taylor is appointed as a permanent manager.
  • 01.12.1959 - Bill Shankly shows faith in Liverpool's coaching team when taking over.
  • 08.07.1971 - Paisley steps up to the role of assistant of Bill Shankly.
  • 26.07.1974 - Takes over as manager of Liverpool.
  • 1976 - Liverpool win the championship, as well as the UEFA Cup. Paisley is voted for the Manager of the Year.
  • 1977 - 2nd championship, Liverpool wins first European Cup for the first time in club's history. Paisley voted for the Manager of the Year.
  • 1978 - Second European Cup.
  • 1979-1980 - two league championship titles.
  • 1981 - Third European Cup
  • 1983 - Liverpool wins the league for 6th time in 9 seasons under Paisley. His manager contract expires.
  • 1992 - Paisley retires from Liverpool's board of directors. He was diagnosed with Altzheimer's disease.
  • 1996 - Bob Paisley passes away.
